Dulce De Leche

Dulce De Leche is a rich, sweet confection made by slowly cooking milk and sugar until it thickens into a thick,creamy, caramel-like sauce with a deep, cooked-milk flavor. A staple in the Latin American cuisine and other parts of the world, this versatile spread or topping is used in desserts like cakes, pastries and ice cream.

Ingredients

  • Whole milk
  • Granulated sugar
  • Vanilla extract
  • Baking soda
  • Pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Prepare the can. Peel all labels off one or more cans of sweetened condensed milk.
  2. Place the unopened cans in a large pot. Fill the pot with water, ensuring the cans are covered by at least 1-2 inches of water.
  3. Bring the water to boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook for to 2-3 hours, depending on your desired color and thickness.
  4. Check the pot frequently (every 20-30 minutes) and add more hot water as needed to keep cans fully submerged.
  5. Remove the cans from the hot water with tongs and allow them to cool completly before opening. This can take several hours.
  6. Lastly, open the can, stir until smooth and transfer the dulce de leche to a jar or airtight container. It will last about 2 weeks in the fridge.
